# UNP_PROJECT_E_COMMERCE_DATA_ANALYSIS_USING_MySQL


I worked on this project in collaboration with UNP, where the main goal was to analyze e-commerce data using MySQL and derive meaningful, actionable business insights. The dataset consists of multiple interconnected tables, including customers, orders, products, and sales details, which provided a comprehensive view of the overall business operations.

To begin with, I structured and explored the data using SQL queries such as SELECT to retrieve relevant information and JOIN operations to combine data from different tables. This helped in building a unified dataset for deeper analysis. I then used GROUP BY and various aggregate functions like SUM, COUNT, and AVG to evaluate performance metrics and identify patterns.

A key part of the project involved analyzing sales performance. I calculated total sales, identified revenue trends, and examined how sales varied across different time periods, including day-wise order patterns and peak sales days. This helped in understanding when the business performs best and where improvements could be made.

Another important focus area was customer behavior analysis. I studied customer purchase patterns, average orders per customer, and identified high-value customers who contribute significantly to revenue. Additionally, I analyzed customers with and without orders to understand engagement levels and potential gaps in conversion.

Product performance analysis was also a major component. I identified top-selling products, most frequently added items to carts, and overall product demand trends. This provided insights into which products drive sales and which may require better marketing or repositioning.

I also explored order-level insights, including payment methods used, full order details, and relationships between customer interest and actual purchases. These insights helped in understanding customer preferences and transaction behavior.

Overall, this project strengthened my ability to work with relational databases and apply SQL for real-world business analysis. It demonstrated how data-driven insights can support better decision-making, improve customer targeting, optimize product strategies, and enhance overall business performance.
